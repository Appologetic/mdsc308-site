Student Safety

Because GSAs exist to promote a safe space for students belonging to a sexual minority, they run the risk of increasing occurrences of discrimination for club members. This means that student safety should be a top priority when creating a GSA.

Here are some tips and tricks from GSA resources that can be used to increase and protect student safety when creating a GSA in your school environment (1-2):

1. Proper education and training for club facilitators (like the "safe adult")
Providing proper training to adults involved in the club on topics like appropriate leadership and crisis management will ensure that the safe adult is safe to be in a position of leadership for a vulnerable group, as well as ensure that they are able to step in and assist in an appropriate way if needed.

2. Respect the privacy of students in and out of the clubBy respecting the right to privacy of the students and not forcing them to join, or participate in discussion if they are uncomfortable doing so, the GSA can remain a safe space for students without presenting any safety concerns in or out of the classroom.

3. Create a clear code of conduct for all club members and "safe adults"
By creating a clear and enforceable code of conduct, and better yet involving the students in it's creation, a safe adult is able to set guidelines for discussion and action that all club members are comfortable with, enabling a safe space with clear consequences for when that safe space is violated.

4. Establishing safe and private meeting spaces
By establishing a space to meet that is public enough that students feel safe being there with an adult, and private enough that no prejudice from students outside the GSA can violate the safe space will enable student safety by limiting the ways that student safety can be violated.

5. Create an emergency plan of action for if/when cases of harassment or other safety concerns occur
In the worst case scenario, having an emergency plan of action for how to deal with instances of harassment, and ensuring that all members are aware of the plan, can ensure that students feel that they are protected and maintain the sanctity and longevity of the safe space even if harassment does occur.
